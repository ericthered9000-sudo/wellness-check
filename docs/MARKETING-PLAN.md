# Wellness Check - Marketing Plan

## App Overview
- **Target Users:** Seniors (primary) + Family Caregivers (secondary)
- **Category:** Health & Fitness
- **Unique Value:** Simple wellness check-in with privacy-first approach
- **Competitors:** Life360, CarePredict, Caring Village (all more complex/expensive)

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-2)

### 1.1 App Store Optimization (ASO) - CRITICAL

**Keywords to Target:**
- Primary: "senior wellness", "elderly care app", "family check-in"
- Secondary: "medication reminder", "wellness tracker", "caregiver app"
- Long-tail: "check in on elderly parent", "senior health tracking"

**Title Optimization:**
- Current: "Wellness Check"
- Consider: "Wellness Check - Senior Care & Family Check-In" (fits more keywords)

**Description Strategy:**
- First 80 chars: Hook + main keyword
- First 500 chars: Value prop + key features
- Use bullet points for scanning
- Include emotional appeal ("peace of mind", "stay connected")

**Screenshots Strategy:**
- Screenshot 1: Wellness score prominently (the main feature)
- Screenshot 2: "I'm Okay!" button (simple value prop)
- Screenshot 3: Emergency 911 button (safety selling point)
- Screenshot 4: Medication reminders
- Screenshot 5: Family dashboard view

### 1.2 Create Marketing Assets

**Landing Page** (can be free on Vercel)
- Hero: "Stay Connected, Stay Independent"
- Value props with icons
- Screenshots/video
- Download buttons (Play Store)
- Privacy-first messaging
- Testimonials placeholder

**Video Demo** (60-90 seconds)
- Show: Open app → See score → Tap check-in → Family sees update
- Emphasize: "One tap. Family knows you're okay."
- End with: "Free to download. Privacy-first."

**Social Media Profiles**
- Facebook Page: "Wellness Check App"
- Twitter/X: @WellnessCheckApp
- Instagram: @wellnesscheckapp
- TikTok: @wellnesscheckapp (for caregiver tips)

## Phase 2: Launch Week (Week 3)

### 2.1 Soft Launch Strategy

**Day 1-3: Friends & Family**
- Get 10-20 friends/family to download
- Ask for honest reviews
- Fix any immediate issues

**Day 4-7: Local Community**
- Post in local Facebook groups
- "Free app for seniors to check in with family"
- Share with senior centers, churches, community groups

### 2.2 Review Generation

**Google Play Review Strategy:**
- In-app prompt after 3rd successful check-in
- "Enjoying Wellness Check? Leave a review!"
- Never ask for 5-star, just ask for review
- Timing: After positive experience

**What to Ask Reviewers to Mention:**
- "Senior-friendly"
- "Easy to use"
- "Peace of mind"
- "Privacy"

### 2.3 Content Marketing Launch

**Blog Posts to Write:**
1. "5 Ways to Check In on Elderly Parents Without Being Intrusive"
2. "The Best Senior-Friendly Apps of 2026"
3. "How to Help Your Parents Age in Place"
4. "Understanding Wellness Scores: What They Mean"

**Guest Post Opportunities:**
- AARP blog (if possible)
- Caregiver blogs
- Senior living community newsletters
- Local newspaper "tech for seniors" articles

**Senior Centers/Communities:**

### 3.2 Partnership Opportunities

**Senior-Focused Organizations:**
- Local Area Agency on Aging
- Meals on Wheels programs
- Senior centers
- Churches with senior ministries
- Assisted living facilities (independent living section)

**Healthcare:**
- Doctor's offices (waiting room flyers)
- Pharmacies (medication adherence angle)
- Home health agencies

**Tech Companies:**
- GrandPad (senior tablet) - partnership?
- GreatCall (senior phones) - partnership?

### 3.3 Paid Advertising (LOW BUDGET)

**Start Small: $50-100/month**

**Google Ads (Search):**
- Keywords: "check on elderly parent app", "senior wellness app"
- Negative: "medical alert", "fall detection" (different market)
- Landing page with strong value prop

**Facebook/Instagram Ads:**
- Target: Adults 40-65 with interest in "caregiving", "elderly care"
- Creative: Before/after - "Worried about Mom? → Peace of mind"
- A/B test: Emotional vs. practical messaging

**Potential Ad Copy:**
```
Headline: "Mom lives alone. You worry."
Body: "Wellness Check lets her tap 'I'm Okay' - and you get peace of mind. Free app. Privacy-first. No tracking."
CTA: "Download Free"
```

### 3.4 Influencer Marketing

**Micro-Influencers (Cheaper):**
- Caregiver bloggers
- Senior-focused YouTubers
- "Sandwich generation" content creators
- Geriatric care managers on social media

**Outreach Template:**
"Hi [Name], I created Wellness Check - a free app that lets seniors check in with family. Would you be interested in trying it? Happy to provide talking points or do a joint video. [Your app link]"

## Phase 4: Retention & Referrals (Ongoing)

### 4.1 In-App Engagement

**Keep Seniors Using:**
- Daily check-in streaks (gamification)
- "Wellness tip of the day"
- Weekly wellness reports
- Medication reminder importance

**Keep Families Using:**
- Push notifications when senior checks in
- Weekly wellness email summary
- "Share with family" button
- Family member referral codes

### 4.2 Referral Program

**Design:**
- Invite 3 family members → unlock premium feature
- Both parties get benefit
- Easy share via text/SMS (seniors use SMS)

**Referral Copy:**
"I've been using Wellness Check to stay connected with family. It's free and simple - just tap 'I'm Okay!' and your family sees you're doing well. [Download link]"

## Phase 5: Advanced Marketing (After 100+ Users)

### 5.1 Press Coverage

**Press Kit:**
- App description
- Founder story (you!)
- Screenshots
- Demo video
- Testimonials
- Contact info

**Press Targets:**
- TechCrunch (small startups section)
- Product Hunt (launch day)
- Hacker News (Show HN)
- Local newspapers
- AARP Magazine
- Caregiver magazines

**Pitch Angle:**
"Built by a 51-year-old Walmart worker learning to code - Wellness Check helps seniors stay independent while keeping families connected. No tracking. No surveillance. Just simple check-ins."

### 5.2 App Store Features

**Apply for Google Play Features:**
- "Designed for Families" badge
- Accessibility features highlighted
- Health & Fitness spotlight

**Google Play Editorial:**
- Submit for "App of the Day" consideration
- Highlight accessibility and senior-friendly design

### 5.3 Growth Hacking

**Viral Loops:**
- Family member invites 2 more family members
- "Invite your siblings" prompt
- Share wellness report on social

**Data-Driven:**
- Track: Which screenshot leads to most installs?
- Track: Which feature keeps users longest?
- Track: Where do users drop off?
- Iterate based on data
